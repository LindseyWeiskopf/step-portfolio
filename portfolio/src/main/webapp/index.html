MY STORY TO CS

When I was in middle school, I learned how to code on my TI-84 calculator because I couldn't remember the quadratic formula. At the time I had no idea that I was programming a small computer, but I realized that I could make my life easier by teaching my calculator something that I couldn't teach myself.

Of course, since then Iâ€™ve programmed more complex machines than a plastic rectangle. And I now understand that when working at its best, CS makes life more accessible for people with different needs, globally.

Through the lens of accessibility, I have sought exposure to the tech field and its critical intersections. By attending talks and panels with industry and industry-adjacent professionals, I have learned about topics beyond the scope of data structures such as GIS, computer vision, and computer architecture. In turn, I am able to apply the information from my professors such as implementing interfaces and data structures, but I am also able to to apply the interdisciplinary information from professionals on the ground to use GIS software and plan workshops about computer vision for ASL users. Whether I am coding on my TI-84 or on terminal, I now understand how integral CS is to our lives.
